Hold price points without dropping perceived quality
▼
Why
Independent operators are watching their highest-volume menu items lose price-to-value alignment. When a burger or an egg dish stops feeling worth what it costs, the whole check average comes under pressure. Helping operators rebuild value here protects the items their business actually runs on.
What
Find products that let an operator hold a price point without dropping perceived quality. Burger build-ups, egg formats, staple proteins.

Protein, reimagined
Proteins that read as a feature, not a substitute
▼
Why
Customer demand is shifting toward protein as a feature rather than meat as a default. Operators who only offer the standard chicken-beef-pork lineup are losing diners who want something smaller, lighter, or non-meat without it feeling like a compromise. The operators who get ahead of this win menu space the rest will be chasing in a year.
What
Find proteins that read as a feature, not a substitute. Plant, dairy, egg-forward, smaller-format.
Who
Hagan, Christina.
Bakery and dessert upgrades
Small upgrades, large perceived-quality gains
▼
Why
Independent operators do not have R&D budgets, and bakery and dessert are where small upgrades produce the largest perceived-quality gains for the lowest cost per cover. A better bun, a better roll, a signature dessert; these are the items diners remember and post about, and they cost the operator pennies to upgrade.
What
Find buns, rolls, breads, and desserts that lift perceived quality at low cost-per-cover.
Who
Matt, and connects back to Tony's burger.

Why
Two real shifts are happening at once: independent operators serving authentic ethnic cuisine are growing in markets like Seattle, and operators across the board are leaning into catering as a margin channel that does not require more dining room seats. SKUs that serve either trend open accounts the standard catalog cannot.
What
Find Hispanic-authentic SKUs and catering-format pack sizes that independent operators can run with.

Packaging and containers
Takeout that holds heat, doesn't leak, doesn't kill margin
▼
Why
Takeout and delivery volume is now structural for independent operators, not a pandemic holdover, and most of their kitchens were never designed for it. Containers that hold heat, do not leak, and do not blow up the food cost are the difference between a third-party order that earns a repeat and one that does not.
What
Find takeout and delivery containers that solve heat, leak, and sustainability without killing margin.
